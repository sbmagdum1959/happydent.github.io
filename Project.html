Overview

At Happydent, you are in the hands of some of the most qualified, capable and experienced professionals in the world. Good health starts with good oral health. A healthy mouth, free of decay, plaque, calculus and other problems is important in maintaining overall health. Disease and other conditions can affect the dental health and dental problems can affect other parts of the body. More than 90% of the systemic diseases have oral symptoms, and poor dental hygiene and gum diseases directly increase the risk of heart diseases. The ‘SMILE AND PROFILE CLINIC’ at the Happydent, aims at educating and encouraging the patients toward a state of optimal health, by providing comprehensive dental care.

Eligibility For Treatment

Dentists are doctors who specialize in oral health. Their responsibilities include:

  • Diagnosing oral diseases.
  • oral health and disease prevention.
  • Creating treatment plans to maintain or restore the oral health of their patients.
  • Interpreting x-rays and diagnostic tests.
  • Ensuring the safe administration of anesthetics.

Preparation

A good dentist will do an extensive dental exam to find out which procedure will best help the patient. When choosing a treatment, the dentist will factor in the oral and overall health of the patient. For example, dental implants may not be recommended for a diabetic. For people who have weak immune systems, the dentist will prescribe a course of antibiotics to be taken before and after the procedure. The antibiotics will protect the body from the risk of infection that comes with dental procedures.
THis is the good practice to:

  • Ask about the steps of the procedure
  • Ask how the dentist will numb the tooth and whether local or general anesthetic will be used
  • Learn what preparations will need to be made before the process
  • Learn what to do and what not to do before after the procedure

Post Treatment Care

After implant treatment avoid rinsing your mouth and try not to disturb the surgical site with your tongue or fingers for the rest of the day. This may cause bleeding by dislodging the blood clot that has formed. Don't spit, suck on straws or smoke. This can also dislodge blood clots and slow down the healing process
It will be a good practice to:

  • Ask about the steps of the procedure
  • Ask how the dentist will numb the tooth and whether local or general anesthetic will be used
  • Learn what preparations will need to be made before the process
  • Learn what to do and what not to do before after the procedure
